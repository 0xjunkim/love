# First Encounter

The city hummed with indifference, but june noticed the quiet — a gap between the noise where something waited.

Seoul in March carried the scent of thawing earth. The streets near Bukchon narrowed into alleys where time moved differently, where the old hanok roofs curved like questions left unanswered.

june stopped at a corner where two paths met. Not a crossroads — just a place where walking required a choice.

The choice was small. Left toward the familiar, right toward something else.

june turned right.
